Hello — I’m Aziz

Undergraduate researcher exploring AI × Clinical Psychology, working-memory modeling (fMRI), ANN MSA, and Behavioral Data Modeling.

I’m a B.Sc. student in Psychology at McGill University (Bashivan Lab). My research focuses on building transformer-based models for cognitive tasks and DNA multiple sequence alignment, as well as developing open-source pipelines for behavioral data modeling. I’m especially interested in aligning artificial neural networks with human brain activity to gain insight into cognition. My long-term goal is to contribute to clinical psychology by using machine learning and neural network models to study how pathologies develop in the brain and at the genetic level.
